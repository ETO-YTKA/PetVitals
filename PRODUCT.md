# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PetVitals is for pet owners and caregivers who manage daily pet care, prepare for vet visits, and coordinate responsibility with trusted people. They need quick access to pet profiles, food and medication routines, care records, and sharing permissions without feeling like they are operating a clinical system.

## Product Purpose

PetVitals is an Android health journal for pets. It helps users maintain accurate, practical care history by tracking pets, records, medications, food, and shared access in one calm workflow. Success means owners can understand a pet's current care state quickly, update it confidently, and share the right information with caregivers when needed.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trustworthy, and approachable. The app should feel like a well-designed care journal: professional enough for health information, friendly enough for everyday pet care, and calm enough for stressful moments before or after a vet visit.

## Anti-references

Avoid sterile medical dashboards, harsh hospital-like interfaces, and cold clinical patterns. Avoid childish or cartoonish pet-app visuals that reduce trust. Avoid dense admin-style screens, cramped tables, and overly technical controls that make routine care feel like data entry work.

## Design Principles

1. Refined simplicity: every element earns its place so pet care details stay easy to scan.
2. Warm professionalism: the interface should feel clean and trustworthy without becoming cold or clinical.
3. Calm hierarchy: highlight the next useful action or status without overwhelming users with records and metadata.
4. Trustworthy interactions: changes to health-related information should feel predictable, reversible where appropriate, and clearly confirmed.
5. Pet-centered care: use organic, humane touches where they support recognition and emotional connection, but never at the expense of clarity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use Android and Material accessibility defaults as the baseline. Maintain readable text, clear focus and state feedback, practical touch targets, meaningful labels for icons and images, sufficient contrast, and interactions that remain understandable with reduced motion or assistive technologies.
